The Story

You know how everyone in India has a sweetu?

Your mom calls you sweetu when she's in a good mood. Your partner calls you that un/ironically. Your best friend screams it across a crowded room and somehow you still turn around even though it's one of the most common nicknames in the country.

Sweetu just has that vibe. The one that makes you feel like someone's person. And if you say it out loud a few times, you'll notice it sounds a lot like "sweet tooth".

So we made an ice cream brand out of it. Because ice cream in this country is a lot more than just the ice and the cream. It's in the "chal, kuch meetha khaate hain" drive-ins at midnight. The tub you open standing in front of the freezer at 1am. The first bite you give someone before they've even asked for it. Ice cream is the softest way to say "I got you" without actually saying it.

Our flavors come from the same place. Things you already love, just frozen for the first time. Chai and Parle-G with actual biscuit bits because dunking is a love language. Rooh Afza and malai to make the summers bearable. Filter kaapi that tastes like actual filter kaapi because we use an actual decoction. Paan with real gulkhand so you feel like you're at a wedding.

Every scoop is three things: milk, sugar, and the real stuff. That's it. No stabilizers, no artificial anything, no ingredients from a chemistry lab.

So when someone asks "what's the scoop?" we can always answer in one sentence. Because there's genuinely nothing but goodness with a twist in there.

What's the Scoop with...

A founder series where the episode is exactly as long as it takes to finish one scoop.

These are conversations with anyone building with Razorpay. The founder who just set up Razorpay on their online store for the first time, the one still pitching to investors, someone like Ritesh Agarwal who's already scaled. Basically anyone who's built something and has a story to tell over a scoop.

So let's say, "What's the Scoop with Ritesh Agarwal" starts on Church Street, Bangalore. He talks about the first OYO listing, what broke, what he'd do differently. The camera is handheld, the pace is the walk, and the melting scoop is the natural timer.

I took inspiration from Razorpay's "Backing India's Boldest" campaign, which gave IPL airtime to founders instead of promoting their own product. We're adding a sweet spin to those stories with real conversations. Think @chickenshopdate or @hotones but warmer, a little more personal, and the ice cream melting is the clock. The medium is a walk and a scoop instead of an ad slot.
Campaign 02
2

Sweetu x ___

Co-branded collabs with cafes, bakeries, and viennoiseries where Sweetu becomes the sweet sidekick to something already yummy to make it even more yummy!

Subko does an affogato with Sweetu's filter kaapi ice cream. Third Wave does a cold brew float with jaggery black sesame. Neon Market makes smoothies with rooh afza malai.

Each collab runs for 2-3 weeks as a limited-run menu item. Co-branded packaging at the cafe. Both brands cross-collab across channels. The customer discovers Sweetu through a place they already love and trust.

Growth angle: This puts Sweetu in the hands of people who already spend on premium food experiences. High-intent consumers who might then go on to seek out Sweetu on their own.

Sweetu for your Sweetu

A quick-commerce play where ice cream becomes the gesture you didn't know you needed.

You're on Blinkit at midnight. You've added your stuff to cart. And then a small banner appears:

Add a Sweetu for your sweetu? 🍨
One tap to indulge that sweet tooth.

The banner appears contextually. Browsing self-care or wellness? "Treat yourself, sweetu." Ordering dinner ingredients at 8pm? "Dessert sorted?" Midnight order? "Late night? You deserve this."

Growth angle: It's the gesture that becomes the ad. If someone already has the intent to buy, we're just intercepting that intent and sweetening the basket.
